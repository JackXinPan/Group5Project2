Sources


After crawling the web for phytopankton data sources, we decided to focus on data provided by the Australian Ocean Data Network. It gives an open access to ocean data with very interesting parameters, like latitude and longitude, but also dates and volumes.

These are very precious elements for us, as we can produce some interesting visualisations like a map and create different kind of plots. It is then much more easy to read, as we are talking about thousands and thousands of data!

We focused on the distribution of Phytoplankton and Zooplankton around Australia in the past ten years, using these two links:

IMOS - AusCPR: Phytoplankton Abundance
IMOS - AusCPR: Zooplankton Abundance
