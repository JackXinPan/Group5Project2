Our website is about the abundance of plankton over time across the coast of Australia. Phytoplankton or “plant drifter” is a catch-all category for all the tiny aquatic organisms that can turn sunlight into food via photosynthesis. While zooplankton or “animal drifter” are also an important part to the ocean ecosystem as they feed on phytoplankton then become a food source for larger predators such as fish. Monitoring the plankton population over time is therefore important to sustain life in the oceans and arguably land as well. Monitoring also provides researchers and policymakers with environmental and climatic indicators, harmful algal bloom detection, eutrophication and pollution information at both local and national scales.
